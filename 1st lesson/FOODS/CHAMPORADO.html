CHAMPORADO
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 kg (about 2.2 lbs) oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 500g (about 1 lb) beef tripe, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch bok choy (or pechay), separated into leaves
  • 1 bundle string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 banana blossom (puso ng saging), sliced and soaked in water with a bit of vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons annatto seeds (achuete)
  • 1/2 cup ground roasted peanuts or peanut butter
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fish sauce (patis), to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
CHAMPORADO TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. In a large pot, combine the glutinous rice and water. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice is cooked and has a porridge-like consistency, about 20-25 minutes.

  2. In a small bowl, dissolve the cocoa powder in a little water to create a smooth paste.

  3. Once the rice is cooked, add the cocoa paste to the pot and stir well to combine.

  4. Add the sugar and salt to the pot, stirring until completely dissolved.

  5. Cook the champorado for an additional 5-10 minutes, stirring frequently, until it thickens to your desired consistency.

  6. Once the champorado is done, remove it from the heat and stir in the evaporated milk. Adjust the sweetness according to your taste preference by adding more sugar if needed.

  7. Serve the champorado hot in bowls, and optionally top each serving with condensed milk or additional evaporated milk. Enjoy!
