HOME FOOD RECIPES USER
SISIG
INGREDIENTS
  • 500g pork belly or pork ribs, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 eggplant (aubergine), sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach), separated into stems and leaves
  • 2-3 pieces green chili peppers (siling haba), optional
  • 1 packet (22g) sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • 8 cups water
  • Fish sauce (patis) or salt, to taste
  • Ground black pepper, to taste
SISIG TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil over medium heat.

  2. Add the pork chunks to the boiling water. Let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes or until the pork is tender and cooked through. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface.

  3. Once the pork is tender, add the quartered onion and tomatoes to the pot. Let them simmer for about 5 minutes to release their flavors.

  4. Add the sinigang mix to the pot. Stir well to dissolve the mix and evenly distribute the flavor.

  5. Drop in the sliced radish and eggplant. Let them cook for about 5-7 minutes or until they start to soften.

  6. Season the soup with fish sauce or salt and ground black pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

  7. Add the kangkong stems to the pot. Let them cook for about 1-2 minutes.

  8. Finally, add the kangkong leaves and green chili peppers (if using). Let them cook for about 1 minute or until the leaves are wilted.

  9. Once all the vegetables are cooked, taste the sinigang and adjust the seasoning if needed. Add more water if the soup is too sour for your taste.

  10. Remove the pot from the heat and serve the sinigang hot with steamed rice. Enjoy!
