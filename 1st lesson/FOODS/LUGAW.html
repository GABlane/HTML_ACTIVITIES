LUGAW
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 cup uncooked white rice (short-grain rice or jasmine rice)
  • 6 cups water (or chicken broth for added flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: Fried garlic, chopped green onions, boiled egg, fried tofu, cooked chicken or pork, fried shallots, calamansi or lemon wedges, fish sauce (patis), soy sauce, or chili flakes
LUGAW TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. Rinse the uncooked rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well and set aside.

  2. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Saute the minced garlic, chopped onion, and sliced ginger until fragrant and the onion is translucent.

  3. Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir to coat the grains with the aromatics.

  4. Pour in the water (or chicken broth) and bring the mixture to a boil.

  5. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the lugaw simmer gently, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking, for about 30-40 minutes or until the rice grains have broken down and the mixture has thickened to your desired consistency. Add more water if needed during cooking to adjust the consistency.

  6. Season the lugaw with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning as needed.

  7. Remove the pot from the heat and ladle the lugaw into bowls.

  8. Serve hot with your choice of toppings, such as fried garlic, chopped green onions, boiled egg, fried tofu, cooked chicken or pork, fried shallots, calamansi or lemon wedges, fish sauce (patis), soy sauce, or chili flakes.
