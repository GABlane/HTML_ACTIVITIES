PAKSIW
INGREDIENTS
  • 500g pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (cane vinegar or white vinegar)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 pieces green chili peppers (siling haba), optional
  • Cooking oil for saut√©ing
PAKSIW TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. Saute Aromatics: Heat a little cooking oil in a pot or deep pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion. Saute until fragrant and the onion becomes translucent.

  2. Add Pork and Simmer: Add the pork chunks to the pot and cook until they are lightly browned on all sides.

  3. Pour Vinegar and Soy Sauce: Pour in the vinegar and soy sauce into the pot. Add water, dried bay leaves, whole peppercorns, and brown sugar. Stir to combine.

  4. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours or until the pork is tender. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to prevent the sauce from drying out.

  5. Add Chili Peppers (Optional): If using green chili peppers, add them to the pot during the last 15 minutes of cooking. This will add a hint of heat to the dish.

  6. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the paksiw and adjust the seasoning with salt if needed. You can also adjust the sweetness or sourness by adding more sugar or vinegar according to your preference.

  7. Serve: Once the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened, remove the pot from the heat. Serve the Pork Paksiw hot with steamed rice.
