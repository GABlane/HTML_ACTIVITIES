TOTANG TALONG
INGREDIENTS
  • 2 large eggplants (talongs)
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil for frying
TORTANG TALONG TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. Grill the eggplants over an open flame or broil them in the oven until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft. Alternatively, you can roast them directly on a stovetop burner until they are tender, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking.

  2. Once cooked, let the eggplants cool slightly. Peel off the charred skin, leaving the stems intact.

  3. Place each eggplant on a flat surface and use a fork to gently flatten and spread out the flesh.

  4. In a bowl, beat the eggs until well combined. Season with salt and pepper according to your taste preference.

  5. In a separate pan, heat some cooking oil over medium heat. Saute the chopped onion and minced garlic until they are soft and fragrant.

  6. If using ground pork or shrimp, add them to the pan with the sautéed onion and garlic. Cook until the meat is no longer pink (for ground pork) or until the shrimp turn pink and opaque.

  7. Pour the beaten eggs into the pan with the sautéed onion, garlic, and optional protein. Mix everything together until well combined.

  8. Dip each flattened eggplant into the egg mixture, ensuring that both sides are well coated.

  9. Heat a clean pan over medium heat and add a little more cooking oil if needed. Place the eggplant into the pan and pour some of the egg mixture over the top.

  10. Cook until the bottom is golden brown, then carefully flip the eggplant over and cook the other side until golden brown and the egg is fully set.

  11. Transfer the cooked Tortang Talong to a serving plate. Serve hot with rice and your choice of dipping sauce, such as tomato ketchup or vinegar with chopped onions and chili peppers.

  12. Garnish with chopped tomatoes and sliced green onions if desired.
