TINOLA
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 whole chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced into strips
  • 2 green papayas, peeled, seeds removed, and cut into wedges
  • 1 bunch malunggay leaves (moringa) or chili leaves
  • 2-3 tablespoons fish sauce (patis), adjust to taste
  • 1 liter water or chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Calamansi or lemon wedges for serving
TINOLA TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.

  2. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and sliced ginger to the pot. Saute until fragrant and the onion becomes translucent.

  3. Add the chicken pieces to the pot. Cook until they are lightly browned on all sides.

  4. Pour in the water or chicken broth into the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to simmer. Let it cook for about 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is tender.

  5. Once the chicken is tender, add the green papaya wedges to the pot. Let them cook for about 5-7 minutes or until they start to soften.

  6. Season the soup with fish sauce (patis) according to your taste preference. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  7. Add the malunggay leaves or chili leaves to the pot. Let them cook for about 1-2 minutes until they are wilted.

  8. Once the vegetables are cooked and the flavors have melded together, remove the pot from the heat.

  9. Transfer the Chicken Tinola to a serving bowl and serve hot with calamansi or lemon wedges on the side.

  10. Enjoy your comforting bowl of Chicken Tinola!
