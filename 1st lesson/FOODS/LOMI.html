LOMI
INGREDIENTS
  • 500g fresh thick egg noodles (or dried lomi noodles)
  • 250g pork belly, sliced into thin strips
  • 250g chicken breast, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 small cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (shiitake or button mushrooms)
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Hard-boiled eggs (optional), sliced
  • Chopped green onions (optional), for garnish
LOMI TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. Heat cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. Saute chopped onion and minced garlic until softened and aromatic.

  2. Add sliced pork belly and chicken breast to the pot. Cook until lightly browned.

  3. Stir in sliced mushrooms and julienned carrot. Cook for a few minutes until vegetables are slightly softened.

  4. Pour in chicken broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes.

  5. Season with soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.

  6. Add fresh thick egg noodles (or dried lomi noodles) to the pot. Cook according to package instructions until noodles are al dente.

  7. Once noodles are cooked, stir in shredded cabbage and the cornstarch mixture. Simmer for another 3-5 minutes until the soup has thickened slightly and the cabbage is tender.

  8. Adjust seasoning if needed. If the soup is too thick, you can add more chicken broth or water to reach your desired consistency.

  9. Serve hot in bowls, garnished with sliced hard-boiled eggs and chopped green onions if desired.
