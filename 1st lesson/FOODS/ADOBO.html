ADOBO
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) chicken pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 cup water
  • Optional: Potatoes, peeled and quartered (about 2 medium potatoes)
ADOBONG MANAK TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. In a large bowl or dish, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, making sure they are well coated. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight in the refrigerator for best flavor.

  2. Heat the cooking oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Remove the chicken pieces from the marinade, reserving the marinade for later. Add the chicken to the hot skillet and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Once the chicken is browned, pour in the marinade and water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is tender and cooked through. If adding potatoes, add them to the pot about 15-20 minutes before the chicken is done, allowing them to cook until tender.

  4. Once the chicken is cooked and tender, remove it from the pot and transfer it to a serving platter. Increase the heat to medium-high and let the sauce simmer uncovered for a few more minutes to thicken slightly.

  5. Pour the sauce over the chicken pieces and serve hot with steamed rice. Adobo is often enjoyed with a side of vegetables or a salad.
