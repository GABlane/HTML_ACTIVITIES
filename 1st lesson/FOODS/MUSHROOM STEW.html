MUSHROOM STEW
INGREDIENTS
  • 500g mushrooms (such as button mushrooms or cremini mushrooms), sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or coconut milk for a dairy-free option)
  • 2 tablespoons butter or olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (optional, for thickening)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley or chives for garnish
MUSHROOM TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. In a large pot, melt the butter (or heat the olive oil) over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and saut√© until they are soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the sliced mushrooms to the pot and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 8-10 minutes.

  3. If using flour for thickening, sprinkle the flour over the mushrooms and stir to coat them evenly. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to cook out the raw flour taste.

  4. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Add the dried thyme, salt, and pepper to taste.

  5. Bring the soup to a simmer and let it cook for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree the soup until smooth. If using a regular blender, be sure to work in batches and vent the blender lid to allow steam to escape.

  7. Return the pureed soup to the pot (if using a regular blender), and stir in the heavy cream (or coconut milk). Let the soup simmer for an additional 5 minutes.

  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. If the soup is too thick, you can thin it out with a bit of water or additional broth.

  9. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley or chives. Enjoy!
