LAING
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 pound dried taro leaves
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup coconut cream (gata)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 tablespoons shrimp paste (bagoong) or fish sauce (patis), adjust to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Optional: Siling labuyo (bird's eye chili), chopped (for added heat)
LAING TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. Prepare the Dried Taro Leaves: Rinse the dried taro leaves thoroughly in water to remove any dirt or impurities. Tear large leaves into smaller pieces if needed. Set aside.

  2. Saute Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a large pan over medium heat. Saute chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant and translucent.

  3. Add Coconut Milk and Water: Pour coconut milk and water into the pan. Bring to a simmer.

  4. Add Dried Taro Leaves: Add the dried taro leaves to the pan, ensuring they are submerged in the coconut milk mixture. Gently press down on the leaves if necessary.

  5. Cook Until Tender: Simmer over low to medium heat for 30 minutes to 1 hour until taro leaves are tender and have absorbed most of the coconut milk. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  6. Add Coconut Cream: Pour in coconut cream (gata) and stir. Simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.

  7. Season with Shrimp Paste or Fish Sauce: Stir in shrimp paste (bagoong) or fish sauce (patis) according to taste. Adjust seasoning as needed. Add chopped siling labuyo if desired.

  8. Serve: Transfer to a serving dish once cooked. Serve hot with steamed rice.
