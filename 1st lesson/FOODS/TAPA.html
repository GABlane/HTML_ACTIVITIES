SISIG
INGREDIENTS
  • 500g beef sirloin or beef flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar (or white sugar)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil (for frying)
TAPA TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, calamansi juice, minced garlic, brown sugar, and ground black pepper. Stir well until the sugar is dissolved.

  2. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade, making sure each piece is well coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or transfer the beef and marinade into a resealable plastic bag.

  3. Marinate the beef in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.

  4. After marinating, heat the cooking oil in a skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat.

  5. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated beef slices to the pan in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. You may need to cook the beef in batches.

  6. Fry the beef slices for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they are browned and caramelized. Be careful not to overcook the beef to prevent it from becoming tough.

  7. Once cooked, transfer the beef tapa to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

  8. Serve hot with garlic fried rice (sinangag), fried eggs (itlog), and sliced tomatoes or atchara (pickled papaya) on the side. Enjoy your homemade Beef Tapa!
