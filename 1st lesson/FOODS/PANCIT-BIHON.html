PANSIT BIHON
INGREDIENTS
  • 250g rice vermicelli noodles (bihon)
  • 200g boneless chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 200g pork belly or pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 small cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup green beans, sliced into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis), optional
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 4 cups chicken broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Calamansi or lemon wedges for serving
  • Chopped green onions for garnish
  • Fried garlic for garnish
PANCIT BIHON TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. Soak the rice vermicelli noodles in warm water for about 10-15 minutes or until they are softened. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a large pan or wok, heat the cooking oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and saut√© until fragrant.

  3. Add the sliced onions and cook until translucent.

  4. Next, add the thinly sliced chicken and pork to the pan. Cook until they are no longer pink.

  5. Once the meat is cooked, add the julienned carrots, sliced green beans, and julienned red bell pepper. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.

  6. Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and fish sauce (if using). Season with ground black pepper. Mix well to combine.

  7. Add the shredded cabbage to the pan and stir-fry until it starts to wilt.

  8. Pour in the chicken broth or water and bring it to a simmer.

  9. Add the soaked rice vermicelli noodles to the pan. Toss and mix well with the other ingredients until the noodles are evenly coated and have absorbed the liquid.

  10. Cook for another 3-5 minutes or until the noodles are fully cooked and most of the liquid has been absorbed.

  11. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more soy sauce or fish sauce according to your taste preference.

  12. Once the noodles are cooked and the flavors have melded together, transfer the Pancit Bihon to a serving platter.

  13. Garnish with chopped green onions and fried garlic on top.

  14. Serve hot with calamansi or lemon wedges on the side. Enjoy!
