KARE-KARE
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 kg (about 2.2 lbs) oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 500g (about 1 lb) beef tripe, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch bok choy (or pechay), separated into leaves
  • 1 bundle string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 banana blossom (puso ng saging), sliced and soaked in water with a bit of vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons annatto seeds (achuete)
  • 1/2 cup ground roasted peanuts or peanut butter
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fish sauce (patis), to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
KARE-KARE TOTURIAL
PROCEDURE

  1. In a large pot, boil the oxtail and beef tripe in water until tender. This may take about 2-3 hours if using a regular pot, but can be shortened if using a pressure cooker.

  2. Once the meat is tender, remove it from the pot and set aside. Reserve the broth.

  3. In a separate pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the annatto seeds and cook until the oil turns red. Remove the seeds, leaving the colored oil in the pan.

  4. Saut√© the garlic and onions in the annatto oil until fragrant and translucent.

  5. Add the ground peanuts or peanut butter to the pan and cook for a few minutes, stirring constantly.

  6. Pour in about 6 cups of the reserved broth (you can adjust the amount depending on how thick you want your sauce). Bring to a simmer and let it cook until the sauce thickens.

  7. Season the sauce with fish sauce (patis), salt, and pepper to taste.

  8. Once the sauce has thickened, add the boiled oxtail and beef tripe back into the pot. Simmer for another 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  9. Meanwhile, blanch the vegetables (eggplant, bok choy, string beans, and banana blossom) in boiling water until tender. Drain and set aside.

  10. Arrange the blanched vegetables on a serving platter.

  11. Transfer the Kare-Kare stew to a serving bowl or platter and serve hot alongside steamed rice and bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side.
